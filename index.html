Biography

Hi! I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at the University of Washington!

My fields of interest are macroeconomics, urban economics, labor economics, and spatial economics, with a focus on migration, labor market, and housing market.

My research examines how household heterogeneity shapes migration motives, migration rates, and the welfare value of internal migration.

Research

Job Market Paper: Who Moves and Who Benefits? The Value of Migration Across Households
I study how migration values and migration rates differ across heterogeneous households using a quantitative life-cycle spatial equilibrium model. I show that migration values and migration rates do not necessarily move in the same direction: wealthier households migrate less but obtain larger migration values. Increased regional productivity dispersion raises migration values and lowers migration rates, with gains concentrated among older and wealthier households.

Presentations: Kyunghee University 2026, WEAI 2026, SEA 2025
Work in Progress: Why Do Educated Households Move to Productive Cities? A Dual Labor Market Perspective
with Kisan Choi

I study the distributional and spatial general equilibrium effects of place-based education investments in an economy with dual labor markets in Korea. I show that brain drain arises even when direct wage gaps across regions are small because regular-job promotion opportunities are concentrated in core regions. Place-based education subsidies in peripheral regions raise local human capital and generate larger welfare gains for low-ability Periphery-born households, even though high-human capital households remain more likely to migrate toward the core.
